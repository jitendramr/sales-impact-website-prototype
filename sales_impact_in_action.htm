What Makes A Sales Professional?

About this module

  • From washing powder to wide-bodied aircraft, Salesmanship takes many forms. Equally, many and various are the Sales Professional’s tasks as they move from the identification of the opportunity, uncovering the Client’s needs, preparing and presenting a viable proposal, to closing the sale.
  • Sales Professionals are business people in their own right and their success lies in their continuing ability to influence the mind of others.
  • Most of us have a Sales background! Beginning during our childhood, we all used sales techniques to influence our parents and peers in attempts to get our own way.
  • In those days we were unselfconscious about asking for what we wanted and ruthless in focusing on establishing our territorial instincts. The skills lasted with many of us up until our initial job interviews that took us into the world of gainful employment. Then, most of us forgot how to sell.
  • Effective selling is the conscious application of a series of techniques that we unconsciously applied in earlier life when we were in our uninhibited, childhood phase. Later in life, with the awareness of adults, many decide that they cannot sell, do not want to or don’t know how too sell.
  • Within this Edulet we set the scene by introducing the broader aspects of the Sales world. Whatever the product or service being offered, there are many basic skills that a Sales Professional must possess as they prepare to increase their skills in the Profession of selling.

Nevertheless, the nature of negotiating and making commercial contracts is by process an act of making a legally binding agreement.
  • Sales Professional's who are to be involved in negotiating commercial contracts need to have a broad grasp of the principles of the law in relation to commercial contracts. The simple process of dealing with a purchase order from a Client will create a legal contract and although this may be a regular and routine process, a clear understanding of the mechanics of creating contracts is an essential part of the Sales Professional's role.
  • This Edulet is not intended to be a comprehensive course on the law of contract, but you will become broadly familiar with the most common aspects of the law of contract and be equipped to negotiate commercial terms of contracts.
  • After completing this Edulet you will be equipped to work with, and have a broad understanding of all aspects of Commercial Business Contracts.
